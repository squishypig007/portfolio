Fake News &
Wellness

An exploration in mental health and user wellness

How did COVID-19 and related news impact your mental health?

As the pandemic began, I felt that the need for mental health advocacy was higher than ever. I, like many others, found myself in search for answers to cope. This led me down a series of internet rabbit holes and anxiety grew, regardless of authenticity of information. The impact of fake news I observed on mental health led me to this project.

Tools Used:

    Survey Monkey, Excel

Existing Research


There is lot of existing research about the prevalence of fake news and its effects on user well-being:


  • In this paper, published by The University of Melboure, the author explains the spike in fake news over the pandemic and its consequences to users
  • It explores the variety of fake news clips that had started circulating in the age of COVID-19, more than ever before
  • The author emphasizes the need to pay attention to the interaction of technology with mental health and wellness



Background

Context

  • For this project, I wanted to explore ways in which the interaction of technology with mental health and wellness was impacted by COV-19
  • This included understanding whether users verify the information they consume and how fake news impacts stress/snxiety levels in emergency situations
  • To do this, I picked a set of fake news clips and created a verify to measure their effects on user wellness

Psychology Considerations

  • Automatic Processing (Model I): Humans have a Dual Model of Processing Information, for effecient and quick interaction, we seek to tap into Model I, ie.e the automatic mode. However, this is also what gets activated during emergencies or in fight or flight mode
  • It also follows that in times of mass uncertainty, any information becomes "important" in the minds of readers and is automatically process
  • The likelihood of users believing what they read in a state of panic as during COVID-19 pandemic is almost three-fold, and the consequences of this are ironically even more anxiety


Process


For my exploartion I took the following steps to further write my paper on "The Effect of Fake News about COVID-19 on Mental Health":

  • I conducted an online survey on SurveyMonkey including a set of 13 questions assessing the concern of individuals regarding the health of their family and the world before and after reading 7 fake news clips about COVID-19.
  • It was not revealed to the participants that these news clips were fake until the end of the survey to eliminate any demand characteristics.
  • This survey was conducted on 315 people - 119 randomly selected individuals from USA and 196 individuals from India having access to weblinks.

Findings


To summarize the findings on my research

  • The mean concern individuals had about themselves/family members prior to exposure to fake news was 6.99, followed by 7.81 after reading the clips. This marked an increase of 11.73%.
  • The mean concern individuals had about world health prior to exposure to fake news was 7.90, followed by 8.31 after reading the clips. This marked an increase of 5.25%.
  • Individuals claimed that they would be 5 times more cautious in handling their pets after reading the fake news clip regarding animals.
  • 52.51% of the participants believed that they were significantly more anxious after reading the given fake news clips.
  • After it was revealed the news clips were fake, 91.35% of the participants reported that they felt that such fake news does have a negative impact mental health.
